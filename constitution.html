UCD Archery Club Constitution

Article 1

The titles, headings and marginal notes in this document are for the purpose of explanations only and do not form any part of this constitution. The singulars shall include the plurals, the masculine shall include the feminine and bodies corporate.


Object and Structure of the Club

Article 2

The Club shall be called the "University College Dublin Archery Club" with its headquarters in the Sports Centre, UCD, Belfield.

Article 3

The object of the Club shall be to instil an abiding interest in archery among its members.

Article 4

The Club shall be affiliated to, or associated with, such organisations as the committee may specify.

Article 5

The Club shall consist of:

  • A President
  • A Captain
  • A Secretary
  • A Treasurer
  • An Equipment Officer
  • 4 Ordinary Members of the committee
  • Ordinary members of the Club.

Article 6

The post of President shall be filled at the invitation of the committee.

Article 7

The committee shall consist of the Captain, the Secretary, the Treasurer, the Equipment Officer (collectively referred to as the "Club Officers") and four ordinary members of the committee.

Article 8

The committee shall meet at least once a month during term time and as often as possible outside term time. The quorum for the committee shall be five, three of whom must be club officers.

Article 9

The Club Officers and four committee members, in addition to any other duties specified in this constitution shall constitute the selection committee who shall choose all the teams to represent the Club and where the need arises interpret this constitution by majority decision.


Responsibilities, Duties and Powers of the Committee and Club Officers

Article 10

  1. The committee shall be responsible for the day to day running of the Club and shall, subject to the provisions of this constitution take such actions and effect such rules, regulations or other instruments as it may deem necessary to realise the object of the club.
  2. Any questions put to a committee meeting shall be decided by a simple majority of the members present. This is subject to the provision that, whereas each member of the committee shall have one vote, the Captain shall have one and a half votes.

Article 11

It is the duty of the committee:

  1. to realise the objective of the Club
  2. to act, if called upon to do so, as a disciplinary committee
  3. to appoint supervisors to marshal all archery sessions and to supervise them through the captain,
  4. to regulate and supervise the opening of the hall to the members
  5. to regulate and supervise the purchase and use by the club of equipment
  6. to fill, by co-option, any vacancies as may occur in the committee between annual general meetings or those positions not filled at the Annual General Meeting
  7. to legislate for any point not provided for in this Constitution
  8. and to interpret this Constitution.

Article 12

The position of President is an honorary appointment by the committee and its incumbent may be reinstated any number of times.

Article 13

  1. It is the duty of the Captain to act as chief supervisor.
  2. It is the duty of the Captain to supervise all other supervisors.

Article 14

  1. It is the duty of the Captain to act as the chair of the committee, to manage the Club and to promote the object of the club at all levels.
  2. It is the duty of the Captain, in consultation with the Committee, to act as Selector to choose all teams to represent the club in competition.
  3. It is the duty of the Captain, in consultation with the Committee, to devise rules for internal competitions and interpret them as deemed necessary.
  4. It is the duty of the Captain to act as the representative of the club at meetings of The Irish Student Archery Association and other intervarsity meetings or to nominate another club member to act on behalf of the club at such meetings.

Article 15

It is the duty of the Secretary to respond promptly to all correspondence of the Club insofar as the particular correspondence is not expressly the province of another member of the committee. It is also his duty:

  1. to report to the committee all correspondence transmitted and received by him.
  2. to record the proceedings of the committee and general meetings.
  3. to preserve in an easily retrievable fashion all important Club documents.

Article 16

It is the duty of the Treasurer:

  1. to attend promptly to all matters relating to the Club's finances.
  2. To report to the committee, and at the annual general meeting to the members present details of all monies received or transmitted by the Club.

Article 17

  1. It is the duty of the Supervisors to instruct and marshal all members in the hall. The Supervisors must comply with the directions of the committee and the Captain as regards safety and security in the hall.
  2. The Supervisors have the power to refuse access to the hall to any person whom they believe presents a threat to safety and security. They may eject from the hall any person who behaves in a dangerous or unseemly manner.
  3. Any such actions referred in Article 17(b) shall be immediately reported to the Captain who shall in turn report this matter to the appropriate College authorities and, if necessary, to the Public authorities.

Article 18

The Captain and Club officers shall within one month of the start of the Michaelmas term make themselves known to the following:

  1. The Agent (UCD Sports Director)
  2. The appropiate College Authorities
  3. The Irish Student Archery Association (ISAA).

Article 19

The Club Officers and the Captain shall each have the power to take actions appropriate to their particular province of administration, without reference to the committee provided that:

  1. Such actions are not contrary to the provisions of this constitution.
  2. Such actions are patently in the interests of UCD Archery Club.
  3. Such actions are reported to the committee within one week of their commission.

Meetings

Article 20

  1. The annual general meeting (AGM) shall be held in the Trinity term. At least twenty-one days notice shall be given to "all members". The following business shall be transacted:
    1. Reading of the AGM minutes
    2. Captain's report
    3. Secretary's report
    4. Treasurer's report
    5. Amendments to the constitution
    6. Election of the committee for the next academic year
    7. Any other business.
  2. Any amendment to the constitution must be presented to the committee a minimum of fourteen days before an AGM. It will then require a simple majority vote of the members present to pass the amendment.
  3. An emergency general meeting (EGM) may be called within term dates if no less than fifteen members forward a written requisition to the Secretary or if the Committee deems it necessary.

Article 21

  1. Should the conduct of any member in any premises occupied by the Club or elsewhere occupied by the Club or elsewhere likely to bring the Club into disrepute, it will be the responsibility of the Club Officers to act as a Disciplinary Committee and convene a meeting. The member must be in attendance to present his case. Failure on his part to attend without good reason will result in his immediate expulsion from the Club. At least seven days notice must be given to the member of such a meeting. It is at the discretion of the committee to serve just disciplinary action.
  2. In paragraph (a) of this Article the term "conduct likely to bring the Club into disrepute" shall particularly refer to conduct likely to endanger the safety or security of others or conduct contrary to the provisions of this constitution.

Article 22

  1. Membership of the Club shall be limited to students and staff of UCD.
  2. Past members, at the discretion of the committee, may also be granted membership.
